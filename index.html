Frequently Asked Questions

How do I add a lens to my collection?

Tap the "+" button in the main view. You can either browse the database of 195+ pre-loaded lenses (tap "Browse Common Lenses"), or manually enter all lens details. When browsing, filter by brand and mount to quickly find your lens.

How do I browse the lens database?

When adding a lens, tap "Browse Common Lenses". Use the brand and mount filters at the top to narrow down results. The database includes 195+ lenses from Canon, Nikon, Sony, Sigma, Tamron, and other major brands. Tap any lens to auto-fill its specifications.

How do I edit a lens?

Tap on any lens in your collection to view its details, then tap the edit button (pencil icon) in the top right. You can modify any information including specifications, purchase details, ratings, and notes.

How do I delete a lens?

In the main list view, swipe left on any lens and tap "Delete". You'll be asked to confirm the deletion. Alternatively, you can mark a lens as "Sold" to keep its records while removing it from active inventory.

What's the difference between marking a lens as sold vs. deleting it?

Marking a lens as "Sold" keeps all its data and history but removes it from your active collection counts and value calculations. Deleting permanently removes the lens and all its data. We recommend marking as sold to maintain your collection history.

How do I rate my lenses?

When adding or editing a lens, scroll to the "Purpose & Rating" section. Tap the stars to rate from 1-5, or tap a filled star to remove the rating. You can also sort your collection by rating to see your favorites first.

How does the coverage analysis work?

Tap the chart icon in the toolbar to view your collection's coverage. The analysis shows: 1) Focal length coverage across your collection, 2) Aperture range visualization, 3) Focal length vs aperture scatter plot, and 4) Gap analysis identifying missing focal lengths or aperture ranges.

How do I track warranties?

When adding or editing a lens, scroll to "Insurance (Optional)" and tap "Add Warranty Expiry Date". Enter the warranty end date and optionally add warranty details. Lenses expiring within 30 days will trigger alerts on the main screen. Tap the shield icon in the toolbar to view all warranty information.

How do I view my collection's total value?

Tap the dollar sign icon ($) in the toolbar to view the Gear Value screen. This shows total purchase value and insured value by currency, plus a sortable list of all lenses from most to least expensive with purchase dates.

Can I track lenses in different currencies?

Yes! GearGrove supports 30+ currencies including EUR, USD, GBP, CHF, JPY, and more. Each lens can have its own currency for purchase price and insured value. The Gear Value view automatically groups and displays totals by currency.

How do I export my collection?

Go to the Import/Export screen (tap the up arrow icon in the toolbar). Tap "Export to CSV" to create a backup file of your entire collection. This includes all specifications, ratings, purchase info, warranty data, and notes. You can share the CSV file via email, Files app, or other services.

How do I import lenses from CSV?

Go to Import/Export and tap "Import from CSV". Select your CSV file. The importer automatically detects duplicates by serial number or brand+model combination and skips them. After import, you'll see a summary of total records, successfully imported, and skipped duplicates.

How do I filter and sort my collection?

Use the filter bar at the bottom of the main screen (iPhone) or left sidebar (iPad). You can: 1) Sort by brand, rating, focal length, aperture, weight, purchase date, or release year, 2) Filter by lens type (Prime/Zoom), brand, mount, 3) Show only weather-sealed or stabilized lenses, 4) Include/exclude sold lenses.

Is my data stored in the cloud?

No. All your data is stored locally on your device. GearGrove doesn't sync to any cloud service, which means your lens collection data stays completely private and secure on your device. You control your data through manual CSV exports.

Is GearGrove optimized for iPad?

Yes! GearGrove includes iPad-specific layouts with: split-screen views showing filters on the left and lens grid on the right, two-column forms for easier data entry, larger lens cards in grid layouts, and optimized coverage charts for bigger screens.

Tips & Best Practices

  • Use the Browse database feature to quickly add lenses with accurate specifications
  • Add serial numbers to help with insurance claims and theft recovery
  • Rate your lenses to identify favorites and underused gear
  • Set up warranty tracking immediately after purchase
  • Use the coverage analysis before planning new purchases
  • Export your collection regularly as a backup
  • Mark lenses as sold rather than deleting to maintain history
